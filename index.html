An Introduction

Rock is a vast genre of music that goes back decades and has a long history. It's amazing to think about how many subgenres of Rock there are and how they came to be. Rock has been evolving since the mid-20th century and has shown up as many different genres. There have been many different supergenres that encompass a lot of subgenres. This informational site will talk about a lot the more popular genres through all of rock history.

Explore each subgenre and find out about its history as well as some notable bands from it. Each subgenre is listed under its parent genre and is in order of when it originated. From Surf Rock to Pop Punk and from Progressive Rock to Shoegaze, a lot of subgenres are organized here to read. Credits are cited on the credits page in the top right.

Hardcore Punk

Originated as a subgenre of Punk, it grew quickly in both the US and the UK. It was originally defined by a lifestyle that is more disciplined and pure but the average perception of Hardcore was a lot more negative because of the aggressive nature of the music. Hardcore has lasted decades from mottos in its community that are along the lines of "Hardcore Will Never Die" and "Forever Hardcore". Being mostly underground, it has lived on longer than any other genre here.

Alternative Rock

After the mid-eighties, Rock couldn't really be considered mainstream and Alternative Rock stemmed from that. It dominated the Rock scene from the mid-eighties to the end of the nineties. It is a collection of a lot of different styles but is usually recognizable by lots of distortion and sentimental lyrics. Songs are longer and slower than other Rock genres with the exception of progressive rock. Alternative Rock died with the end of the millenium but lives on in the form of Indie Rock.

Contemporary Rock

Contemporary Rock encompasses all new Rock genres in the 21st century. A lot of genres in this era share characteristics but not in the form of technology but rather a way of forming these genres. These genres were formed from taking old genres and forming completely new ones out of them. Contemporary Rock is defined by the building of bridges to other musical genres.
